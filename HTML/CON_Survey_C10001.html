Condition Survey:

Structural PriorityNone

Aesthetic PriorityLow

Storage/Display ConditionsGood

ConditionThe painting is structurally sound and in good condition.  It has been alleged that the sky is extensively repainted.  A full laboratory examination is needed to determine the true state of the picture.
2012: There is no apparent change to the condition of the painting.  The old cracks that were previously noted appear to remain stable,
2013: There is no apparent change to the condition of the painting.  The old cracks that were previously noted appear to remain stable.
2014: No change to painting, small areas of lifting (gilding and gesso) on frame located at the inner edges and bottom left area.
2015:  No changes to painting or frame, areas of lifting in the frame gilding remain unchanged.
2016: No change to the painting. Minor consolidation was done to the minor areas of lifting on the frame using Lascaux medium for consolidation.
2017: No changes to painting or frame.

Primary SupportThe wood panel is stable and in plane.

Secondary SupportThe verso was not available for inspection but there is no reason to suspect any problems with a cradle.

Ground/Preparatory LayerThe (assumed) white gesso layers are in stable condition.  There are many old vertical cracks that are sound at this time.

MediaThe (assumed) egg tempera and mordant gilding layers are in stable condition.  There are many old vertical cracks that are sound at this time.  The paint is worn and abraded in some areas and tiny old losses that were never retouched are visible but the painting is in acceptable visual appearance at this time.  The extent of old repainting and restoration can only be determined with a full laboratory examination.  

Surface CoatingsThe painting is evenly coated with a semi-gloss varnish that is acceptable in appearance at this time.  There are scattered small dull spots, and a small white area is visible at the top of the (red) side of the "door." 

Recommendations/NotesA full laboratory examination is needed to determine the true state of the picture.
